    Contraceptive Usage by Age and Language

    The charts reveal significant differences in contraceptive use between English and Spanish native speakers, with the gap widening as they age. Among younger women, English speakers show higher rates of modern contraceptive use, likely due to greater access to education and healthcare. Spanish speakers in this age group tend to have lower usage rates, influenced by cultural norms or limited access. However, as women grow older, the disparity becomes even more pronounced, with English speakers continuing to show high usage rates while usage among Spanish speakers remains relatively low. This growing gap highlights potential systemic barriers or cultural factors impacting older Spanish-speaking women.
